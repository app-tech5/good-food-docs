Good Food — Restaurant App

The companion app for restaurant owners and staff: manage incoming orders, edit the menu, track performance, and keep the kitchen in sync with the rest of the Good Food suite.

This overview explains what the restaurant app does, how it fits next to the customer and driver apps, which platforms it supports, and what your backend must expose before staff can sign in.

Easy to launch Android & iOS ready Connects to your Good Food API

Overview (Presentation)

The restaurant app is the operations side of Good Food. It is used by owners and staff to process incoming orders, maintain menus, and track business metrics.

It connects to your REST API (same backend stack as the other apps), and authenticates with restaurant credentials before exposing orders, menu management, and reporting screens.

Customer app — Overview Driver app — Overview

Main function

Centralize restaurant workflows in one mobile app: accept and update orders, edit menu items, and monitor performance indicators in real time.

The app uses providers and hooks for auth, orders, menu, stats, and settings. It also includes smart cache helpers to keep screens responsive while data refreshes in the background.

How navigation is organized

Startup flow: Splash → Login → main drawer after authentication. The drawer then exposes dashboard, orders, menu, analytics, reviews, reports, notifications, support, profile, and settings.

Dedicated stacks handle deeper flows such as order details, menu add/edit, reports, reviews, profile, and settings subpages.

Configuration & demo mode

Runtime configuration lives in config.js (API URL, timeout, demo credentials, feature flags, and business defaults).

In demo mode, auth, orders, and menu actions can return mock data to speed up demos without a full backend setup.
